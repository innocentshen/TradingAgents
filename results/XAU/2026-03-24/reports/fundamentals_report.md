### Analysis of Gold (XAU) as an Asset Class
While traditional equity-based fundamental analysis is not possible for XAU, gold is analyzed using different factors:

*   **Monetary Policy and Interest Rates:** Gold is a non-yielding asset. It generally performs well in low-interest-rate environments and inversely to real interest rates (as real rates rise, the opportunity cost of holding gold increases).
*   **Inflation Hedging:** Gold is historically viewed as a store of value and a hedge against currency devaluation and significant inflation.
*   **Geopolitical Risk:** Gold is a "safe-haven" asset; its demand typically increases during periods of geopolitical instability, economic uncertainty, or market volatility.
*   **Supply and Demand:** Gold prices are influenced by physical demand (jewelry, industrial usage, central bank buying) and supply (mining output and recycling).
*   **Currency Strength:** Gold is priced in US Dollars. There is a strong historical inverse correlation between the value of the US Dollar (USD) and the price of gold.
