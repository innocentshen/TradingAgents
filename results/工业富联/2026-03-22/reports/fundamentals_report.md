# Fundamental Analysis Report: Foxconn Industrial Internet (601138.SS)

**Date of Report:** 2026-03-22

## 1. Company Profile
Foxconn Industrial Internet Co., Ltd. (FII) is a major player in the global technology manufacturing sector, specializing in the production of communication equipment, cloud service devices, and precision tools. As a key subsidiary of the Hon Hai Precision Industry Co. (Foxconn), it holds a dominant position in high-tech manufacturing, serving as a critical partner to many of the world's leading technology brands.

## 3. Financial Performance Analysis (2021-2025)

### Income Statement Trends
The company has demonstrated impressive top-line growth over the past five years. Total revenue increased from ¥511.85 billion in 2021 to ¥902.89 billion in 2025, reflecting the expanding demand for AI infrastructure, cloud computing, and high-performance networking equipment.

Profitability has also followed a positive trajectory. Net income increased from ¥20.07 billion in 2021 to ¥35.29 billion in 2025. While gross profit margins remain relatively thin—a characteristic typical of large-scale electronics manufacturing—the company's ability to maintain these margins while significantly scaling production indicates strong operational efficiency and cost management.

### Balance Sheet & Debt
As of year-end 2025, the company's total assets stood at ¥456.22 billion, up from ¥284.19 billion in 2021. This expansion is supported by substantial investments in property, plant, and equipment (PPE), which increased from ¥18.72 billion in 2021 to ¥37.46 billion in 2025, highlighting the firm’s commitment to expanding capacity to meet high-tech demands.

The company's debt position warrants scrutiny. Total debt has grown significantly, reaching ¥110.18 billion in 2025, up from ¥60.32 billion in 2021. While the ROE of 22.06% indicates effective use of equity, investors should monitor the debt-to-equity ratio as capital expenditures continue to rise.

### Cash Flow Analysis
A notable shift occurred in 2025 regarding cash flow. While the company has historically generated strong operating cash flows, the free cash flow for 2025 was negative at -¥11.99 billion. This was largely driven by significant increases in working capital requirements—specifically, inventory buildup and accounts receivable—as well as sustained high levels of capital expenditure. Traders should watch closely for a reversal of this trend in upcoming quarters to ensure that the heavy investment in capacity translates back into sustainable free cash flow.

## 5. Investment Insight
Foxconn Industrial Internet exhibits strong growth in revenue and net income, fueled by its critical role in the global tech supply chain. Its high ROE indicates efficient utilization of capital. However, the recent negative free cash flow in 2025, coupled with a notable increase in total debt, suggests a period of intensive capital investment that requires careful monitoring. Investors should evaluate whether the current capital expenditure will yield sufficient returns to service the growing debt and return to positive free cash flow, especially given the competitive and low-margin nature of the contract manufacturing industry.
