### Analysis for Traders
Since XAU represents a commodity (Gold) rather than a company, it does not have earnings, debt, or cash flow metrics that typical fundamental analysis relies on. Instead, trading XAU is generally driven by:

*   **Macroeconomic Factors:** Gold is often used as a hedge against inflation and currency devaluation. It tends to perform well when economic uncertainty is high.
*   **Interest Rates:** As a non-yielding asset, the price of gold is inversely related to real interest rates. When interest rates rise, the opportunity cost of holding gold increases, potentially pressuring its price.
*   **Geopolitical Stability:** Gold is considered a "safe-haven" asset. Increases in geopolitical tensions often lead to increased demand for gold as a store of value.
*   **Central Bank Policies:** Significant buying or selling by central banks can have a material impact on the supply and demand dynamics of gold.
