**Date of Analysis:** 2026-03-23
**Subject:** XAU (Gold)

### Overview
It is important to clarify that **XAU** is the ISO 4217 currency code used to represent one troy ounce of gold. Because XAU is a commodity and not a publicly traded corporation, it does not issue traditional corporate financial documents such as Balance Sheets, Income Statements, or Cash Flow Statements.

### Financial Analysis Limitations
Traditional fundamental analysis—which involves examining a company’s revenue growth, profit margins, debt levels, and cash flow—**cannot be performed on XAU**.

When evaluating commodities like gold, traders do not look at financial statements. Instead, they analyze macroeconomic factors, including:
*   **Central Bank Policies:** Interest rates and monetary policy decisions by major central banks (e.g., the Federal Reserve).
*   **Inflation Data:** Gold is often viewed as a hedge against inflation.
*   **Geopolitical Stability:** Global uncertainty often drives demand for gold as a "safe-haven" asset.
*   **Currency Strength:** The strength of the US Dollar (since gold is primarily priced in USD, a weaker dollar often makes gold more attractive).
